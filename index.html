What is wikonnect?
Wikonnect, seeks to bridge the digital divide through provision of digital literacy skills.

Wikonnect is a cooperation project between the Strategic Partnership Digital Africa (SPDA) and the Kenyan digital company BRCK (https://www.brck.com/). The project is funded by the German Federal Ministry for Economic Cooperation and Development (BMZ).

Learning

Technology is changing the world and education needs to adapt. Wikonnect lets you learn valuable skills at your own pace, anytime, anywhere.

Content

Often, our most important teachers aren’t from school. They are our parents, family, friends, colleagues, bosses, and others. Wikonnect enables us to share knowledge with our communities online, extending the reach of our most valuable teaching relationships into the digital world.

Open Source

What did you learn today?

The free digital content on Wikonnect helps you develop skills and a digital reputation. The content is a mix of videos, animations, audio, and text. It is organized into topics with quizzes and other ways of testing your knowledge.

Content can be created by anyone in the world. What did you build today?

Open source software makes the digital world go around. “Open source” means that the source code of our platform is freely-available for anyone to copy and for anyone to add to or modify. Our dedicated community of open source software volunteers is headquartered in Nairobi.

Our Partners

GIZ

Wikonnect is a collaboration between the Strategic Partnership Digital Africa (SPDA), and the Kenyan digital company BRCK. A network of the German development cooperation and 200 European companies with the goal to initiate public-private co-operations in the digital sector in African countries. The project is funded by the German Federal Ministry for Economic Cooperation and Development (BMZ) and is coordinated by the Deutsche Gesellschaft für Internationale Zusammenarbeit (GIZ) GmbH.

Learning Lions

Learning Lions (LL) is a gUG non-profit organizations based in Germany and Kenya which creates digital content for Wikonnect. LL also runs training programs derived from TI’s 3-month intensive training program in tech, design, and business in remote Kenya.

Tunapanda Institute

Tunapanda Institute (TI) is a 501(c)3 non-profit organization based in the US and Kenya. TI builds and maintains the core Wikonnect software through our team members and skilled open source volunteers. TI also establishes and funds training programs in low-income areas, such as Kibera.

BRCK

BRCK is a top African technology hardware company, headquartered in Nairobi. BRCK helped conceive the Wikonnect platform and generously provides internet distribution to its 500,000 monthly active users as well as Moja Points, which give people access to wifi on busses and other hotspots, to users of the platform.
